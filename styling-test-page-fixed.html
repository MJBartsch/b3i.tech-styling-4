How Do We Assess Trust and Quality of Crypto Betting Sites?

Bitcoin betting sites should offer the hottest odds on a wide range of sports. When it comes to our evaluation criteria, sites must tick several boxes to be classified as one of the best.

Selection of Cryptocurrencies

The more cryptocurrencies a bookie supports, the more options you have. This makes it easy to use your preferred coin for deposits and withdrawals.

Speed of Withdrawals

Since crypto transactions take place on the blockchain network, deposits and withdrawals are instant. Reliable crypto betting sites will match this, paying out your winnings within a few hours.

Transaction Fees Applied

The decentralised nature of cryptos means they don't come with transaction fees. Some may have mining fees, which top bookies absorb for you instead of applying fees of their own.

No Verification Requirements

Unique regulations apply to Bitcoin and other altcoins that don't include KYC checks. This means top crypto bookies won't ask you for heaps of personal documents before you can access your winnings.

Quick Registration

Another key feature of leading Bitcoin betting platforms is their short and sweet sign-up process. Most won't ask for more than an email address and a password.

Betting Limits

An identifying feature of reputable crypto bookies is above-average betting limits. These sites make it easier to wager high-stakes bets on a variety of sports events.

Why Should You Bet with Crypto Instead of Traditional Payment Methods?

While traditional payment methods provide familiarity, Bitcoin and other cryptos have far more to offer.

Are Crypto Betting Sites More Secure Than Traditional Bookmakers?

Bitcoin and other altcoins are highly encrypted digital currencies with transactions taking place on a blockchain network. With no third-party involvement and instant transactions, deposits and withdrawals on Bitcoin sportsbooks are some of the safest.

Traditional payment methods require sharing card details or bank account information. Crypto transactions use your wallet address—a string of characters that reveals nothing about your identity or finances.

Do You Really Get Anonymous Betting with Cryptocurrency?

Since cryptocurrencies aren't tied to stringent regulations, you won't need to supply Know Your Customer verification documents or provide personal details. You can bet online anonymously from anywhere across the globe.

Your betting site knows you're depositing and withdrawing, but the blockchain itself only records wallet addresses, not personal identities. This provides a layer of pseudonymity unavailable with credit cards or bank transfers.

Are Crypto Transactions Really Instant and Fee-Free?

By processing all transactions on a blockchain network, cryptos can offer instant, fee-free transactions, so you receive all your winnings in your pocket within a few hours.

Most crypto betting sites charge zero deposit and withdrawal fees, compared to 2-5% fees for credit cards and e-wallets. Even when blockchain network fees apply (typically £0.50-£5 per transaction), they're lower than traditional payment fees.

Do Crypto Betting Sites Offer Better Bonuses?

On average, the value of crypto coins is far greater than that of traditional currencies, which means Bitcoin betting sites can offer more for your money through impressive bonuses.

According to YouGov, one in seven UK bettors are interested in joining crypto betting sites. It isn't hard to see why when looking at their impressive high-value promotions worth thousands rather than the typical £500 bonuses.

What About Betting Limits with Cryptocurrency?

Since they're not restricted by financial institutions, crypto bookmakers offer more extensive limits on transactions and betting. Many top bookies will allow you to deposit as little as £1 and as much as £100,000.

High maximum limits make it easier to wager high-stakes bets on a variety of sports events. Traditional payment methods often cap single transactions at £10,000-£25,000.

What Are the Drawbacks of Crypto Betting?

Betting with Bitcoin has a host of benefits, still, there are a few drawbacks worth noting:

  • Limited Availability – Despite the increasing popularity of crypto as a payment option, not all online bookmakers offer this option.
  • Increased Volatility – Unlike traditional currencies, cryptocurrencies are volatile. A large payout today may not be worth as much tomorrow, which makes some bettors cautious about using crypto for gambling.
  • Extra Steps – To use Bitcoin to fund your crypto betting account, you'll need to follow a few additional steps, like setting up a crypto wallet and purchasing coins.

How Can You Bet Responsibly with Cryptocurrency?

Privacy and security are two top features of Bitcoin betting sites, but ultimately, your online safety still rests with you. There are several things you should and shouldn't do if you want to stay safe when betting at crypto sites.

Do:

  • Use a Trusted Crypto Wallet – Pick a wallet that has a good reputation. You can check out user reviews on forums to find top options. Also, rather store funds in your crypto wallet rather than in your betting account.
  • Stick to Coins You Know – There are regularly new altcoins entering the market, many which are touted by influencers. However, these are highly volatile and carry great risk, making it safer to stick to coins like Bitcoin and Ethereum which are trustworthy.
  • Set Up 2-FA or Biometric Sign-in – Unfortunately, there is always a chance of online fraud. This makes it essential to set up two-factor authentication or biometric sign-in on your betting account, which makes it challenging for hackers to access your information.
  • Gamble Responsibly – Practice responsible gambling by staying within your means. Setting a budget, applying limits to your betting account, or reaching out to problem gambling resources are the best ways to stay safe.

Don't:

  • Use Public WiFi – Hackers often broadcast WiFi networks that imitate public WiFi networks. If you accidentally join one of these false networks, any details or login information you share while using that network become available to the hacker.
  • Share Your Wallet Address with Just Anyone – While sharing your wallet address is relatively safe, sharing your private keys or other key details about your crypto wallet can leave you vulnerable to hackers.

Pro Tip: Rather set up a WiFi hotspot from your mobile device if you want to use the internet in a public area. This requires you to input a specific password, which is much safer.
